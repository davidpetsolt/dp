Bunks

Where we crash

Two bases: Deep Ellum to bookend the trip, the East Side for the heart of it.

Kimpton PittmanDallas · nights 1-2
MapSite
Boutique Kimpton in the heart of Deep Ellum, built next to the restored 1916 Knights of Pythias Temple and named for its architect William Sidney Pittman, the first Black architect registered in Texas. Heated outdoor pool to wash off the flight, and the BBQ and live-music blocks are right outside the door. Our Dallas base on the way in.
Heywood HotelAustin · nights 3-5
MapSite
A tiny seven-room inn on East Cesar Chavez with a MICHELIN Key and free parking, steps from Flat Track coffee and a short walk to the Cesar Chavez BBQ row. No pool, so the swimming happens at Barton Springs. The most walkable, characterful base in town, and where the trip really lives.

The Hat

Built to last

A classic lid that doubles for Safari later: wide-brim felt or straw, natural tan or chocolate, minimal trim. The Fort Worth Stockyards is the better hat country, so this is now a Wednesday stop, with an Austin fallback if you'd rather do it there.

The Best Hat StoreFort Worth · Stockyards
MapSite
Walk-in. Thousands of hats, all American Hat Company, shaped to your head on the spot. The quality pick.
M.L. Leddy'sFort Worth · Stockyards
MapSite
Stockyards institution since 1941. Pick a felt or straw and they crease and fit it to you. Boots and leather too.
Longhorn Fashions Hat BarAustin · fallback
MapSite
The Austin option, more decorate-your-own: pick the felt, shape the brim, choose the band. Walk-in, near UT.
Water

Cool off

Late August runs hot. A spring-fed swim, a rooftop deck, or a full float down the Guadalupe.

Tube the GuadalupeNew Braunfels · ~45 min
Map
Rent through Rockin' R, rope the tubes together, float a cooler between you. Cold dam-fed water. If the river's low, switch to the spring-fed Comal. Eat at the Gristmill in Gruene before or after.
Barton Springs PoolAustin

Three acres of 70° spring water with a skyline view. A few dollars to get in.

Map

Roads

Where to drive

The Bronco is the classroom. Big highway miles between cities, twisty Hill Country once you're settled.

Dallas to Austin, the back wayUS-281
Map
The pretty way down: two-lane miles through Glen Rose (dinosaur tracks and the Fossil Rim drive-through safari), Hico, Lampasas, and Burnet. Calmer and better scenery, but about 4.5 to 5 hours, so it needs a day with no clock. With the Thursday spa, the run down is the faster I-35.
Around AustinHill Country
Map
RR 2222 and RR 620 around Lake Travis, Hamilton Pool Road to Dripping Springs, and the climb up Mount Bonnell. The calmest, prettiest miles, and the best place for Cameron to actually log practice.
The Twisted SistersSkip this trip
Map
RR 335/336/337 west of the Hill Country — legendary, but tight switchbacks two-plus hours out. Save it for a future run.
